About H2E

1/3 - Biomass Gassification

Biomass gasification is a thermochemical process that converts biomass feedstocks, such as agricultural waste, forestry waste, energy crops, or organic waste materials, into a gas mixture known as synthesis gas. The prepared biomass is fed into a gasifier, which is a high-temperature reactor where thermochemical reactions occur. In the gasifier, the biomass is heated in an oxygen-limited environment,typically with steam or air, to produce a synthesis gas, also known as syngas. The syngas produced in the gasifier contains Carbon monoxide (CO), Hydrogen (H2), Carbon dioxide (CO2), Methane (CH4), and various impurities such as tar and particulate matter. to obtain a high-quality syngas suitable for hydrogen production, the gas stream goes through a series of cleaning steps. These steps and other impurities though processes like filtration, scrubbing, and catalytic conversion.

3/3 - H2Nose

An array of gas sensors is used to detect the composition of the air. Each gas sensor has its own gas to detect: H2, CO, CO2 and CH4. Combined with an AI identification system, it can record data from previously burned pellets. The AI looks for a unique pattern similar to a fingerprint from the analyzed air. This unique pattern is obtained by the gas sensors. Later on, if a similar fingerprint is identified, it will be able to tell which pellet was burned.
